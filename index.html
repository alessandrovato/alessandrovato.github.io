Interfacing with the Human Mind

Welcome to the digital home of BCIReahb Lab. We specialize in developing high-fidelity, adaptive brain-computer interfaces that bridge the gap between biological intention and digital execution. Our goal is to restore autonomy through neural engineering.

About the Lab

Our methodology is built on the triad of Respect, Listening, and Adaptation. We treat the brain not just as a signal source, but as a living partner in a digital dialogue. By solving for "interface leaks," we ensure long-term stability in neural implants and non-invasive sensors alike.

Led by a multidisciplinary team of neuroscientists and engineers, we focus on the rehabilitation of motor and sensory functions post-injury.

Research Focus

Our research pillars are designed to push the boundaries of what is possible in Neural Rehabilitation:

  • Signal Processing: Developing ML algorithms that filter noise from high-density EEG/ECoG data.
  • Neuroplasticity: Studying how the brain rewires itself when engaged with a BCI over long periods.
  • Haptic Feedback: Creating bidirectional interfaces that "talk back" to the nervous system.

Active Projects

Project: Synapse-X
