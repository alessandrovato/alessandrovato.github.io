Research Pillars

1. Signal Acquisition & Pre-processing

We investigate high-density electrode arrays and non-invasive EEG methods to capture the most robust neural signals possible. Our focus is on reducing "noise" in real-world environments.



2. Computational Neuroscience

Our lab develops advanced mathematical models to understand how the brain encodes movement and sensation. We use these models to build faster, more intuitive decoders.



3. Long-term Interface Stability

A major hurdle in BCI is the biological response to implants. We research materials and adaptive software that can maintain a high-quality connection for years, not just months.
