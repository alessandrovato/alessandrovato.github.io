Brain-Computer Interface for Rehabilitation Laboratory

Our Mission

In the Fallingwater House, artificial and natural elements are bonded together in harmony, creating one of humankind’s most beautiful masterpieces. After more than 80 years, the water still doesn’t give up. Moisture, flooding, and leaks are part of the daily relationship between nature and man’s manufacture. To obtain and keep harmony, the artificial continuously needs to respect, listen, and adapt to the natural. Research in Brain-Computer Interfaces shares the same goal (harmony), the same issues (“leaks” in the electrode-brain interface), and the same method (respect, listening, adaptation).

Our Philosophy

We believe that the brain is not just a signal source, but a living partner in a digital dialogue. Our methodology is built on a triad of:

  • Respect: Prioritizing the biological integrity of the neural system.
  • Listening: Developing higher resolution sensors to capture the brain's intent.
  • Adaptation: Creating BCIs that evolve as the user learns and recovers.

Location & Affiliation

Located within the **Catholic University of America**, we collaborate with clinicians and engineers to move laboratory breakthroughs into real-world rehabilitative settings.
